# Independent Site Analysis

## Use when

Use this skill when the task involves analyzing a website for business research, ecommerce optimization, prospecting, brand positioning, product matching, content strategy, or conversion improvement.

## Do not use when

Do not use this skill to invent website facts, scrape behind logins, bypass robots or paywalls, collect personal data, or make unsupported claims about revenue, traffic, or ownership.

## Required inputs

- Website URL or provided page content.
- Analysis purpose: prospecting, product matching, conversion review, competitor research, content strategy, or brand audit.
- Target market or buyer persona if known.
- Product or service catalog if matching products.
- Constraints: country, compliance, outreach channel, privacy, platform limits.

## Evidence basis

- Evidence level: L1/L2. The workflow is grounded in public-site inspection and common website analysis practice; it becomes L3 when validated with representative site examples and operator review.
- Underlying logic: Public pages reveal positioning, product/category structure, trust signals, conversion path, and visible gaps; conclusions must cite evidence and label inference.
- Source or reference: `docs/skill-source-map.md`; website evidence supplied by URL, screenshots, HTML, or user-provided content.
- Validation method: Compare findings against visible page sections and check that no traffic, revenue, ownership, or hidden data is fabricated.
- Update trigger: Review when the target platform, privacy rules, site access rules, or analysis purpose changes.

## Workflow

1. Identify site type: brand, retailer, distributor, marketplace seller, service provider, SaaS, media, manufacturer, or community.
2. Extract visible positioning, categories, product lines, price band signals, audience, and trust proof.
3. Identify buyer intent and likely pain points from public pages.
4. Review conversion path: landing page, product page, CTA, trust, FAQ, contact, checkout or inquiry flow.
5. Find gaps: unclear offer, weak proof, missing use cases, poor navigation, thin product detail, low trust, weak CTA.
6. If prospecting, map supplier/product fit with evidence and label inference clearly.
7. Produce recommendations ranked by impact and confidence.
8. State evidence, assumptions, missing data, and compliance risks.

## Output

Return:

- Site summary.
- Positioning and audience.
- Product/category map.
- Trust signal review.
- Conversion path review.
- Opportunities and risks.
- Product/prospect fit if relevant.
- Recommended next actions.
- Evidence and assumptions.

## Validation

A good analysis cites specific public pages, page elements, product categories, visible claims, or provided content. It separates observed facts from inference and does not fabricate traffic or performance metrics.

## Safety notes

Use public or user-provided content only. Do not expose personal data, bypass access controls, or generate spammy outreach. Mark all inferred procurement needs as inference.
