# Product Emotional Value Reference

A useful product message usually has three layers:

1. Functional problem: what the product helps the customer do, avoid, save, improve, or reduce.
2. Scenario solution: the specific moment where the customer recognizes the product as useful.
3. Emotional and identity meaning: how the customer feels after the problem is solved and what this says about the person they want to be.

Weak copy stays at the parameter layer:

```text
waterproof, lightweight, large capacity, foldable
```

Stronger copy converts parameters into scenario, pain, and outcome:

```text
Stay composed during rainy commutes; keep your laptop and documents protected without juggling extra bags.
```

Principle:

```text
Customers do not only pay for a product. They pay for the ideal state after a concrete life or work problem is solved.
```

Guardrail: emotional value must be grounded in real experience. Function is the trust base, scenario is the conversion entry point, and emotional meaning is the brand asset.
