# Ecommerce Product Emotion

## Use when

Use this skill when a user needs product positioning, product detail page copy, ad angles, landing-page messaging, content seeding, brand storytelling, or feature-to-benefit translation.

## Do not use when

Do not use this skill to invent fake customer stories, fake reviews, fake certifications, unsupported performance claims, medical/legal/financial claims, or claims not grounded in the product.

## Required inputs

- Product name and category.
- Target customer.
- Use scenario.
- Product specs and constraints.
- Channel: product page, ad, landing page, social post, email, marketplace listing.
- Brand tone.
- Proof points: reviews, tests, certificates, materials, warranty, delivery, before/after evidence.

## Workflow

1. Extract functional value: what concrete problem does the product solve?
2. Map scenarios: when, where, and why does the customer use it?
3. Identify pain points: inconvenience, anxiety, wasted time, embarrassment, uncertainty, risk, or friction.
4. Translate specs into outcome language.
5. Add emotional value: relief, control, confidence, comfort, care, freedom, pride, identity.
6. Separate verified facts from marketing inference.
7. Create channel-specific variants.
8. Flag risky or unsupported claims.

## Output

Return:

- Feature-to-benefit table.
- Scenario map.
- Pain-point map.
- Emotional and identity value angles.
- Proof needed for claims.
- Copy variants by channel.
- Risky claims to avoid.

## Validation

A good output makes the buyer feel a concrete scenario and outcome, not just read product parameters. It should preserve verified facts and label inference.

## Safety notes

Do not exaggerate. Emotional value must be built on real product experience. Function is the trust base, scenario is the conversion entry point, and emotional meaning is the brand asset.
