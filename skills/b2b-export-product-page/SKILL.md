# B2B Export Product Page

## Use when

Use this skill for B2B product pages, wholesale listings, factory product catalogs, supplier landing pages, RFQ pages, Alibaba-like listings, and international buyer inquiry flows.

## Do not use when

Do not use this skill to recommend keyword stuffing, invent factory capacity, fake certifications, fake buyer cases, false compliance, or unsupported delivery promises.

## Required inputs

- Product category and product name.
- Target buyer: importer, distributor, brand, wholesaler, retailer, contractor, manufacturer, or procurement team.
- Product specs and variants.
- MOQ, customization, packaging, lead time, certificate, warranty, and logistics details if available.
- Target countries or industries.
- Current title, attributes, images, and description if available.
- Competitive or inquiry data if available.

## Workflow

1. Identify buyer type, procurement scenario, and purchase decision criteria.
2. Separate search keywords, technical attributes, use cases, compliance needs, and trust proof.
3. Review title, category, attributes, images, specification table, packaging, MOQ, customization, lead time, and certificates.
4. Diagnose funnel bottleneck: exposure, click, trust, inquiry conversion, inquiry quality, or sales handoff.
5. Rewrite product content around buyer intent and procurement confidence.
6. Add missing sections: applications, specs, customization, quality control, packaging, logistics, FAQ, certificate proof, inquiry CTA.
7. Flag unsupported claims and missing evidence.
8. Define validation metrics: exposure, CTR, inquiry rate, qualified inquiry rate, sample requests, RFQ relevance.

## Output

Return:

- Buyer and scenario diagnosis.
- Title or listing rewrite.
- Product page section structure.
- Specification and trust-proof checklist.
- Inquiry CTA and FAQ suggestions.
- Missing data and risks.
- Validation metrics.

## Validation

A good B2B page improves buyer confidence and inquiry quality, not only traffic. Recommendations must be grounded in product facts and buyer procurement logic.

## Safety notes

Do not expose customer data. Do not invent certificates, factory scale, delivery time, test reports, clients, or compliance status. Mark assumptions clearly.
